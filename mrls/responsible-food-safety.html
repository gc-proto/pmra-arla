Pesticides, maximum residue limits and food safety: Who is responsible for food safety in Canada

  • Overview
  • Maximum residues around the world
  • Calculating, setting and adjusting MRLs
  • Who is responsible for food safety in Canada

Responsibility for food safety is shared between:

  • food producers
  • federal departments and organizations

Health Canada:

  • evaluates the health and environmental risk of proposed pesticides before they can be approved or used on foods
    • part of this evaluation includes analyzing pesticide residues
  • handles food recalls
  • provides food safety tips for all food types
  • provides information on how to protect those who are vulnerable to food poisoning and food allergies

Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada and Health Canada:

  • work together on programs to help provide healthy food for people in Canada
  • engage food producers in sustainable pest management practices and encourage environmental protection

The Canadian Food Inspection Agency:

  • enforces federal food safety regulations for both domestic and imported food
  • samples domestic and imported foods for chemical residues and pesticide residues
    • If tested food products exceed limits, enforcement actions could include:
      • removing the food from stores
      • seizing food stocks
      • rejecting imports
      • prosecuting offenders

They also make sure producers:

  • properly use and apply pesticides
  • respect established residue limits

Food producers help keep the food supply safe by using and improving on good agricultural, food safety and sustainable pest management practices. This, in turn, helps keep residues within the limits.

Contact us

For information about:

  • inspection, monitoring, and surveillance of food, contact the Canadian Food Inspection Agency at 1-800-442-2342
  • agricultural practices, contact Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada at 1-855-773-0241
  • pesticides or MRLs, contact the Health Canada Pest Management Information Service at 1-800-267-6315
