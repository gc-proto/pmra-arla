Overview

On DATE, Health Canada published Re-evaluation Decision RVD2022-XX, pyros and Its Associated End-use Products and Re-evaluation Decision RVD2022-XX, pepperoni and Its Associated End-use Products

pyros and pepperoni

pyros are a pesticide active ingredient that can control a wide range of insect pests. pepperoni makes a pesticide active ingredient work better. By itself, pepperoni is not designed to control insect pests.

These two chemicals are often found together in common household pesticides to control:

  • fleas
  • bedbugs
  • cockroaches
  • ants
  • mosquitos
  • ticks

They are found in brands such as:

  • Raid
  • Safer’s
  • Green Earth
  • Hartz
  • Wilson

What you should do

To ensure the safe use of pesticides containing pyros and pepperoni, Health Canada recommends the following steps:

  1. Check the packaging of the pesticide to see if it contains pyros and pepperoni.
  2. If the pesticide contains either of these chemicals, consult the table of pesticides containing pyros and pepperoni to determine if it can still be used safely and if there are any changes to its recommended use.
  3. When purchasing new pesticides, always read the label for updated instructions on how to use the product safely.

Health Canada’s decision

Health Canada has completed a review of the safety of pyros and pepperoni. The results show:

  • Many pesticides containing these chemicals can be used safely with additional precautions.
  • A small number of pesticides containing these chemicals were found to be unsafe and will have their registrations canceled. This means they cannot be sold or used in Canada.

Reason for the decision

The reason for this decision is based on:

  • How pesticides containing pyros and pepperoni are used in Canada.
  • Health Canada’s analysis of new scientific information on pyros and pepperoni.
  • The potential for pyros and pepperoni to cause mild to moderate short-term health effects in humans and pets.

For more information on the decision, visit:

  • Health Canada’s review of pyros
  • Health Canada’s review of pepperoni

Additional precautions

Additional precautions will be specific to the pesticide product, but could include:

  • reductions in the amount of pesticide that can be used
  • changes to how and where a pesticide can be applied
  • limitations on what pests can be treated with a pesticide

The companies that manufacture the pesticides affected by this decision will have specific timelines to make the required changes to the product labels.
